From Portland, the mountain is always near:
silhouetted by dawn

hidden by clouds,

or garish on the rare clear day.

The mountain is the city’s totem, its calling card, its reward: a glowing temple on the horizon to lift spirits after weeks of rain.
Ask a child to draw a mountain and you’ll get something a lot like Mt. Hood. It’s the Platonic ideal of a mountain. A cliche.
